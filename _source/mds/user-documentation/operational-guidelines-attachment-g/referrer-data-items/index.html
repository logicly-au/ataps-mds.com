Data Items to be collected by the GP or referrer on a patient referral form - Sociodemographic and clinical characteristics of patients

    Data items to be collected for all programs

    Item Options Type Notes
    Patient Key   FK - Patient Key

    Each patient should be allocated a 'patient key' which is a unique code to de-identify a patient, allowing him/her to be followed across his/her sessions with the mental health professional, without the evaluators ever needing to know his/her name. Ideally, these codes should be generated and inserted on the form by the Medicare Local, and not by the GP or referrer.

    Required.
    Birth year   Year

    When combined with 'Date of referral', 'Year of birth' allows age to be calculated. This provides more information than if respondents were asked to tick an age band (e.g., 20-29), and allows for subsequent data aggregation for comparison with Census data.
    Gender   FK - Gender

    Allows for comparisons with Census data. Indicate the gender of the patient from the list of radio buttons.
    Statistical Linkages Key   SLK

    As documented at the Australian Government Metadata Online Registry (METeOR), the purpose of the SLK is to maintain greater accuracy of patient data, within and across Medicare Locals. The SLK provides the capacity to identify individual clients across time in order to support more accurate reporting of repeat vs new clients being serviced. Ideally, the SLK should be generated and inserted on the form by the Medicare Local, and not by the referrer.

    Patient data can be entered without an SLK. However, you can not add a referral dated on or after 1st of June, 2013 unless the patient does have an SLK..
    Does the person speak a language other than English at home? Language spoken at home FK - Language This question is useful in providing a description of those who are accessing mental health care. It is asked in the same format as on the Census, to allow for comparison.
    Language other Text
    How well does the person speak English?   FK - English Level

    This question is useful in providing a description of those who are accessing mental health care. It is asked in the same format as on the Census, to allow for comparison. Two response categories (i.e., not well and not at all) imply that the person will need further services in a language other than English. Indicate the english level from a drop down menu.
    Is the person of Aboriginal origin?   FK - YesNoUnknown

    This question is useful in providing a description of those who are accessing mental health care. It is asked in the same format as on the Census, to allow for comparison. For persons of both Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander origin, select 'yes' for both categories.
    Is the person of Torres Strait Islander origin?   FK - YesNoUnknown

    This question is useful in providing a description of those who are accessing mental health care. It is asked in the same format as on the Census, to allow for comparison. For persons of both Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander origin, select 'yes' for both categories.
    Program   FK - Program

    This field was introduced in December 2010 to distinguish between the Tiers of programs. Some 'referral types' have specific data options available to them within the minimum dataset. Please select this option carefully as some of the other minimum dataset items are dependent on your selection here.

    Required.
    Referrer code   FK - Referrer or MHPro code

    This is a unique code that is used to de-identify the individual referrer (previously known as 'provider'). Indicdate the referrer from a drop down menu, or if the referrer doesn exist in the menu enter a new code.

    Required.
    Referral date   Past Date

    As noted, this can be combined with 'Birth Year' to allow age to be calculated. It also provides information on any delays in access to subsequent care.

    Required.
    Referrer type   FK - Referrer Type

    The referrer type for General ATAPS is GP. However, some of the Tier 2 services have other allowable referrers. These will be presented to you as a drop down menu option.

    Not required, default is 'GP'.
    Postcode of the patient's residence   Postcode

    Residential postcode, combined with the postcode of the Referrer's practice, can provide information about the distance that people travel to receive care. Information on socio-economic indicators and on rurality can also be gleaned from residential postcode. Local evaluations may add more detailed questions to describe area of residence in rural areas where a single postcode may cover a substantial area.

    Required if the referral date is on or after 1st of June, 2013.
    Postcode of the referrer's practice   Postcode

    As noted for 'Patient's postcode', postcode of the referrer's practice, combined with residential postcode, can provide information about the distance that people travel to receive care.

    Required if the referral date is on or after 1st of June, 2013.
    Does the person live on his/her own?   FK - YesNoUnknown

    This question relates to the important issue of the role of others in transacting care. Supplementary questions in the local evaluation may provide further information in this regard.
    Is the person on a low income?   FK - YesNoUnknown

    This question is useful in providing a description of those who are accessing mental health care. It requires the Referrer to make a judgement that takes into account comparative levels of income and evidence such as health care card or pension status.
    What is the highest level of education the person has completed?   FK - Education Level

    This question is useful in providing a description of those who are accessing mental health care. It is asked in a format that can be mapped to the Census, for comparison purposes. Indicate the education level from a drop down menu.
    Has the person ever received specialist mental health care before (public/private, medical/mental health)?   FK - YesNoUnknown

    This question relates to any episode of illness - not just the one for which the person is currently being treated.
    Additional Sessions   Boolean

    This box is ticked if the patient is accessing between 12 and 18 sessions.
    If a referral has concluded, in what way has it concluded?   FK - Conclusion

    Once this field is completed, the referral is deemed to have concluded and therefore no further information can be entered for this patient, for this referral. Select the conclusion from a drop down menu.
    ICD-10 primary care diagnostic categories Unknown Boolean These categories represent the ICD-10 Chapter V Primary Care Version Brief Version (with amended categories) and are the minimum required. Projects may chose to include the Concise Version (with amended categories) as described in the Guidelines for Submissions to Conduct Pilots eg F10 Alcohol use disorders F11 Drug use disorders etc. Multiple responses permitted.
